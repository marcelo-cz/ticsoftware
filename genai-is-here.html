GenAI is Here: Are You Ready to Leverage It for NonStop?

The Generative AI Revolution for NonStop

The Generative AI revolution has arrived at a critical juncture for the NonStop community. As organizations grapple with modernization challenges, aging technical workforces, and the imperative to preserve mission-critical knowledge, GenAI offers unprecedented opportunities to bridge Nonstop systems with future requirements.

At TIC Software, our two years of focused research into Generative AI applications for NonStop environments has culminated in TIC Navigator, unveiled at Connect TBC 2025. This solution combines deep domain expertise with cutting-edge AI capabilities, specifically engineered for Nonstop's unique architectural demands.

Addressing Critical NonStop Challenges

TIC Navigator directly tackles the three key concerns plaguing most NonStop shops:

  • Scarce NonStop expertise in the marketplace
  • Retiring subject matter experts holding decades of institutional knowledge
  • Modernization barriers due to incomplete understanding of existing code logic

By leveraging AI to extract and preserve business logic embedded in COBOL, TAL, C/C++, and TACL codebases, TIC Navigator transforms implicit system knowledge into accessible documentation that enables both immediate operational improvements and strategic modernization initiatives.

GenAI at TBC 2025

The enthusiasm for GenAI within the NonStop community was unmistakable at TBC 2025, with organizations across the ecosystem searching for practical AI implementations. Casey Taylor, GM and SVP of HPE, presented the Nonstop AI-Strategic Journey, which aligns perfectly with what TIC Navigator delivers today. Besides TIC Navigator, vendors like NTI and Idelji also presented on the topic of AI. HPE demonstrated potential GenAI toolsets for database and monitoring.

Our long-time client Pape Group presented their successful experience of using GenAI in documenting their COBOL codebase. This momentum reflects a broader industry recognition: GenAI is no longer experimental—it's operationally ready and delivering measurable value in production environments.

Japan Leads the Way

Perhaps most significantly, the Japanese NonStop community has emerged as an early adopter of GenAI. At TBC 2025, both TIS and JRI presented comprehensive AI initiatives:

TIS launched the Nonstop Community for Generative AI Utilization in Japan, recognizing that the critical shortage of Nonstop talent requires collective action. This community-driven initiative demonstrates strategic thinking about GenAI's role in preserving institutional knowledge and enabling sustainable development practices.

Japan Research Institute complemented this approach by showcasing practical GenAI applications using tools like Cursor.

We applaud the foresight of Japan's Nonstop community in forming such forums. We hope that HPE and the US Nonstop community will follow Japan's lead in forming a similar collaborative GenAI community. TIC would certainly look forward to participating in and contributing our knowledge and expertise to benefit the entire NonStop ecosystem through shared learning and innovation.

The Knowledge Preservation Imperative

Traditional knowledge transfer methods are insufficient when institutional memory is stored in so many decades of developed codebase. The Nonstop community faces this unprecedented challenge compounded by the gradual retirement of experienced Subject Matter Experts (SME).

GenAI offers a transformative solution by capturing, codifying, and making accessible the deep technical knowledge embedded in Nonstop systems. TIC Navigator was built to address these challenges with AI-powered analysis that extracts business logic, architectural patterns, and operational procedures that would otherwise be lost.
